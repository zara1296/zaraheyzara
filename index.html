The cat (Felis catus) is a domestic species of small carnivorous mammal. The cat is similar in anatomy to the other felid species: it has a strong flexible body, quick reflexes, sharp teeth and retractable claws adapted to killing small prey.

Its night vision and sense of smell are well developed.

The genus Felis diverged from the Felidae around 6–7 million years ago.

The domestic cat is a member of the Felidae, a family that had a common ancestor about 10–15 million years ago. These are the most popular cat types.

  • Persian cat
  • British shorthair
  • Maine Moon
  • Sphynx cat

    • The origin of the English word 'cat', Old English catt, is thought to be the Late Latin word cattus, which was first used at the beginning of the 6th century.
